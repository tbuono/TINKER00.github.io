The Climate Is Changing. So Must Buildings.

scroll down

Cities like Boston are becoming more aware of the impacts that building architectures can have on sustainability initiatives, and altering policy and building codes to try and ensure a greener future.

In 2007, Boston instated Article 37, requiring all major building projects (over 50,000 square feet) to meet specific sustainability and resiliency requirements, accredited by institutions such as LEED.

Knowing that Boston is currently undergoing an explosion of development, our team seeks to determine whether there has been a marked change in certain “green” building typologies since 2007, by visualizing building permit data from before and after Article 37.
